The Nigerian Civil War (1967–1970), also known as the Biafran War, was fought between Nigeria and the secessionist state of Biafra.

The war was caused by ethnic tensions, political instability, and military coups in 1966. The Eastern Region, led by Odumegwu Ojukwu, declared independence as Biafra in 1967.

Yakubu Gowon was the Head of State of Nigeria during the war.

Major events included the declaration of Biafra, military offensives, and a blockade that caused severe famine.

The war ended in January 1970 when Biafra surrendered.

The effects included millions of deaths, starvation, displacement, and long-term political consequences in Nigeria.
